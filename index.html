About Project

Built to reveal why attendance changes, not just how much.

The project simulates realistic academic schedules, exam windows, holidays and student behavior to identify the contextual factors that influence classroom participation.

Why it matters

Attendance often reflects stress, scheduling friction and social behavior before it becomes an academic performance problem.

Problem statement

Raw attendance logs hide patterns across weekdays, courses, exams, holidays and groups of students.

Objectives

Clean data, engineer signals, validate trends statistically and convert analysis into recruiter-ready visual insights.

Expected outcomes

Actionable dashboards for policy evaluation, counseling, course comparison and early risk detection.

Features

Analytics modules that feel production-ready.

Attendance Analytics

Analyze attendance percentages, absenteeism patterns and semester trends.

Attendance Heatmaps

Spot high and low participation windows across subjects and weekdays.

Student Behaviour Analysis

Identify frequent bunking behavior and contextual participation changes.

Statistical Analysis

Use Chi-Square, ANOVA, descriptive statistics and correlation analysis.

Network Graph Analysis

Reveal co-absence relationships and attendance communities.

Trend Detection

Track presence shifts before exams, after holidays and across courses.
